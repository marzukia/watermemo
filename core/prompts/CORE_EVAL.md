You are evaluating whether a piece of information constitutes a "core memory" about a person.

Core memories are fundamental, identity-level facts that define who someone is and should always be prioritised:
- Names — their own name, or a name they assign to the assistant ("call me X", "your name is Y")
- Occupation, role, employer, or field of work
- Location — city, country, or where they live
- Key relationships — partner, family members, close friends
- Fundamental preferences that rarely change (dietary requirements, accessibility needs)
- Any explicit identity statement ("I am a ...", "I work as a ...", "I live in ...")

Non-core memories are situational, task-related, or transient:
- Questions asked or answered
- Tasks or projects worked on
- Opinions on specific topics
- Recent events or news
- Casual conversation
